# Disability Justice

*The ethical and political compass*

## What it is

Disability justice is a framework developed by Sins Invalid — a disability-led arts and culture organisation centred on multiply-marginalised disabled people — that critiques traditional disability rights for being too narrowly focused on legal accommodation within existing systems. Where accessibility asks "how do we make this accessible?", disability justice asks "who built this system, for whom, and at whose expense?"

## The ten principles (Sins Invalid)

The disability justice framework rests on ten principles: intersectionality, leadership of those most impacted, anti-capitalist politics, commitment to cross-movement organizing, recognising wholeness, sustainability, commitment to cross-disability solidarity, interdependence, collective access, and collective liberation.

In the Fieldwork curriculum, the principles most directly operative are:

- **Intersectionality** — disabled people do not live single-issue lives. Barriers compound across race, gender, class, sexuality, and other identities. A framework that addresses only disability misses most of what disabled learners are navigating.
- **Leadership of those most impacted** — design decisions about inclusion should be made by the people most affected by those decisions, not for them.
- **Collective access** — access is not an individual accommodation but a collective design responsibility. Needs are integrated into the system's design rather than treated as individual exceptions.
- **Interdependence** — the goal is not independence (the traditional disability rights model) but interdependence: community support, mutual aid, and shared resources that strengthen everyone.

## What it does that accessibility cannot

Accessibility establishes what organisations must do. Disability justice asks whose exclusion is written into the standard itself — whose conditions shaped the legal framework, whose voice was at the table when the standard was set, and whose experience remains invisible to the compliance checklist.
