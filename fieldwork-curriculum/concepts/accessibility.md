# Accessibility

*The legal floor and the essential infrastructure*

## What it is

Accessibility, in the Fieldwork framework, refers to the foundational baseline: the removal of physical, technological, and attitudinal barriers to ensure equitable participation in learning environments. It is the first pillar — not because it is the most important, but because nothing else can rest on a foundation that isn't there.

## What it requires

Accessibility requires moving from reactive retrofitting (adding accommodations after the design is complete) to proactive, universal design (building access into the design from the start). This includes:

- **Legal compliance** — Accessible Canada Act (ACA), Accessible Ontario Disabilities Act (AODA), Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA). These establish the floor.
- **Technical standards** — WCAG 2.1 Level AA for digital content: perceivable, operable, understandable, robust.
- **Proactive design** — Universal Design for Learning (UDL) as the architecture, not an add-on.

## What it does not require

Accessibility does not require perfection. It requires honesty about where barriers exist, a commitment to addressing them proactively rather than reactively, and a recognition that compliance is the beginning of the conversation, not the end.

## What it cannot do alone

A system can be fully accessible by every legal and technical standard and still fail to include. This is the foundational problem the curriculum addresses. Technical accessibility ensures that content can be reached — it does not ensure that it is culturally relevant, pedagogically useful, or designed with the understanding that learners do not live single-issue lives.

This is why disability justice and emergent learning are not add-ons to accessibility — they are the conditions under which accessibility becomes genuinely inclusive.

## Key terms

- **Legal floor** — the minimum standard of compliance established by legislation. The starting point, not the destination.
- **Proactive design** — building accessibility in from the beginning rather than retrofitting it afterward.
- **Universal Design for Learning (UDL)** — a framework for providing multiple means of representation, engagement, and expression so every learner can reach the same goal by a route that works for them.
- **Retrofitting** — adding accessibility after the design is complete. Always more expensive and less effective than proactive design.
