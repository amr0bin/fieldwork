# The Three Pillars

Every post is located within one primary pillar. The pillar shapes the angle.

## How to use this

Each week is tagged with one pillar. The pillar tells you which of the three moves is primary that week — and what the subheading's intellectual weight is resting on. The pillars are not sequential. They are interdependent. A post tagged Disability Justice still has infrastructure and pedagogy running underneath it. A post tagged Accessibility still has to ask whose interests the standard serves.

---

## Accessibility

*The legal floor and the essential infrastructure.*

Accessibility weeks focus on what the system currently does or is required to do — the technical standards, legal frameworks, and proactive design practices that create the conditions for equitable participation. The primary question: what does it actually take to remove barriers before they occur?

**Primary move:** Ground (what the system currently does or fails to do)

**Key concepts:** WCAG 2.1, ACA, AODA, ADA, Universal Design for Learning, proactive design, retrofitting, the "legal floor"

**Weeks:** 1, 2, 3, 7, 8, 13, 22

---

## Disability Justice

*The ethical and political compass.*

Disability Justice weeks focus on whose interests the current design serves and who it misses. Rooted in the framework developed by Sins Invalid, this pillar centres intersectionality, the leadership of those most impacted, collective access, and interdependence over independence. The primary question: who is written out of the standard itself?

**Primary move:** Whose interests? (who leads, who benefits, who is most impacted)

**Key concepts:** Intersectionality, collective access, interdependence, leadership of those most impacted, social model of disability, co-design, positionality

**Weeks:** 4, 5, 9, 14, 15, 17, 20, 23

---

## Emergent Learning

*The pedagogical method.*

Emergent Learning weeks focus on what becomes possible when the design is made adaptive, relational, and context-driven. Drawing on critical theory, Paulo Freire's pedagogy, complexity theory, and neuroeducation, this pillar asks what it means to treat learning as a living system rather than a delivery mechanism. The primary question: what conditions facilitate emergence rather than control a fixed outcome?

**Primary move:** What becomes possible (adaptive, relational, context-driven design)

**Key concepts:** Emergent learning, neuroplasticity, active learning, spaced repetition, collaborative inquiry, iterative experimentation, backward design, reflection-in-action

**Weeks:** 6, 10, 11, 12, 16, 18, 19, 21, 24

---

## Why the three are inseparable

You cannot achieve **Collective Access** without the **Technical Compliance** to make it possible. You cannot sustain **Emergent Learning** without the **Reflective Practice** to guide it. You cannot do any of it equitably without the **Intersectional Analysis** to understand who is actually in the room and what they are navigating.

This is what distinguishes the practitioner-researcher from the content creator: the capacity to hold the structural, the relational, and the dynamic in active, simultaneous relationship — not as separate phases, but as one integrated practice.

| Cluster | Pillar | Core focus |
|---------|--------|------------|
| **Structural** | Accessibility | Technical compliance · proactive design · backward curriculum design |
| **Relational** | Disability Justice | Intersectional analysis · collaborative stakeholder engagement · collective access |
| **Dynamic** | Emergent Learning | Methodological inquiry · facilitating emergent adaptation · neuroeducational literacy · iterative reflective practice |
