## Writing notes for this template

**On the threshold sentence:** It is a pivot, not a recap. The reader spent a week with the Part 1 concept. They don't need it restated. They need to feel the shift from understanding to doing.

**On the Try section:** The activity must be doable in the reader's actual context — not an idealised institution with resources. If the activity requires a budget, a team, or a policy change, it is not ready. Start simpler. The adaptation note at the end gives permission to modify; make sure that permission is genuine, not a disclaimer.

**On the Watch section:** The video exists to demonstrate a skill or show a practice in action — not to add length or authority. If it would not change what the reader does, it does not belong.

**On the Share prompt:** The difference between a weak and strong prompt: "Share your thoughts on co-design" vs. "Map the stakeholders in your current project. Circle the ones who were involved before design decisions were made. Bring that map to your next team meeting and ask: what would need to change for the circled people to be in the room earlier?" The second prompt is specific enough that the reader knows exactly what to do, and open enough that different contexts will produce different conversations.

**On the Reflect section:** This is not a summary and not a preview. It is where the post earns its place in the field's ongoing inquiry. What do we still not know? What are practitioners across contexts still working out? The reader's own practice might contribute to answering it — write from that position.
