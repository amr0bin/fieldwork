# Positionality Statement

*Who is conducting this inquiry, and from where*

Positionality is not a disclaimer. It is a methodological tool. Naming the researcher's relationship to the research — their identities, their access, the limits of what they can see from where they stand — makes the inquiry more honest, not less rigorous.

---

## Context

This fieldwork is conducted by Andrea Robin, an inclusive learning design practitioner based in Vancouver, BC. Accessible Learning Labs is a practice and public inquiry platform focused on what it means to design for the full range of human variation — from the beginning, not retrofitted afterward.

The inquiry is located at the intersection of professional practice and public scholarship. It is practitioner-research: generated from inside the work, with and for the communities the work is meant to serve.

## Relationship to the subject

The researcher works professionally in learning design across post-secondary, public sector, and community contexts. This provides direct access to the policy-practice gap the inquiry investigates, and it also creates specific blind spots: the researcher's institutional contexts shape which gaps are most visible to them and which remain outside their line of sight.

## Ongoing accountability

Positionality is not settled at the beginning of the inquiry. As the research develops, the researcher's relationship to the material and to participants changes, and the positionality statement should be updated to reflect that.
