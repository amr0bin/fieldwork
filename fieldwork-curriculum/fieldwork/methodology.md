# Methodology Notes

*How the inquiry is structured and why*

## Research design

Fieldwork uses a mixed-methods approach:

**Qualitative strand** — participatory interviews, focus group facilitation, observational research, and document analysis. Purpose: to understand what exclusion feels like from inside it, to hear what structured questions don't anticipate, and to hold the specificity of individual experiences without flattening them into themes.

**Quantitative strand** — survey design and analysis, institutional data review. Purpose: to understand the scale and pattern of problems, to test whether qualitative findings hold across larger populations, and to produce evidence that is legible to institutional decision-makers.

The two strands are treated as complementary, not competing. Quantitative data reveals the shape of the problem. Qualitative data reveals what it is like to be inside it.

## Ethical framework

Ethics is treated as an ongoing relationship rather than a one-time process. Specific commitments:

- Ongoing informed consent — participants can renegotiate their consent if the research direction changes
- Transparent confidentiality protocols — participants know exactly how their data will be used
- Non-extractive practice — the inquiry asks what participants and their communities need from it, not only what the research needs from them

## Reflective practice

The inquiry uses Donald Schön's two modes of reflection:
- **Reflection-in-action** — noticing and adjusting during the research process
- **Reflection-on-action** — reviewing and learning after the process

Both are documented in the fieldwork archive and shape the ongoing development of the research question.
