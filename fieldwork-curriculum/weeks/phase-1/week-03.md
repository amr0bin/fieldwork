# Week 3

**Pillar:** Accessibility  
**Phase:** Foundations & Thesis Discovery

---

## Heading — the provocation

**The systems designed to carry knowledge are also the ones that drop it.**

---

## Three subheadings — the moves

**1. What the system currently does**  
Most knowledge delivery systems — LMS platforms, training modules, institutional repositories, onboarding sequences — are designed with an assumed participant. That assumed participant has reliable internet, a current device, English as a first language, no significant cognitive or sensory difference from the norm, and access to a distraction-free environment. When the system drops knowledge, it drops it for the person furthest from that assumption.

**2. Whose interests that serves**  
Assumed-participant design is efficient and cheap. It also systematically excludes the same people every time — not randomly, not accidentally, but structurally. The system is not failing; it is doing exactly what it was designed to do for the people it was designed for.

**3. What becomes possible**  
An environmental scan that maps where the system carries knowledge and where it drops it is the first honest research act. It names the design, rather than the learner, as the problem.

---

## Part 1 brief

**Why:** Introduce the environmental scan as a research practice. Explain the concept of the "assumed participant" — the person whose conditions the system was built around. Reframe: when a system consistently drops the same people, the problem is in the design, not the learner.

**See:** A knowledge system — a corporate LMS, a post-secondary course, a community literacy program — that was redesigned after an environmental scan revealed whose participation had been assumed rather than designed.

---

## Part 2 brief

**Try:** Map the knowledge delivery systems currently in use in your environment. For each one, ask: who does this system assume? Where does it drop support? Who consistently has to work around it?

**Share:** Share your map with a peer. Where do you see the same gaps?

**Reflect:** What would need to change about how your organisation attributes responsibility if the system, rather than the learner, were named as the source of the drop?
