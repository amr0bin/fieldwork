# Week 6

**Pillar:** Emergent Learning

## Heading — the provocation

**A proposal that can't change isn't a blueprint — it's a plan.**

---

## Three subheadings — the moves

**1. What the system currently does**  
Research proposals in most institutional contexts are treated as binding commitments: this is what you will research, this is how, this is why. The value of the proposal is its specificity and its finality. Changing the direction mid-process is seen as a failure of planning.

**2. Whose interests that serves**  
A fixed proposal serves institutional oversight — it creates a clear record of what was agreed. It does not serve emergent inquiry, which by definition produces findings that couldn't have been predicted at the outset. When the research finds something unexpected, a fixed proposal has no mechanism for following it.

**3. What becomes possible**  
A proposal treated as a living blueprint — specific enough to begin, responsive enough to change — creates the conditions for genuine emergent inquiry. It names the research question without foreclosing what the research can discover.

## Part 1 brief

**Why:** Introduce the concept of the living blueprint — a research proposal specific enough to begin, responsive enough to follow what it finds. Distinguish between proposals designed for institutional approval and proposals designed to guide genuine inquiry.

**See:** A case where a practitioner-researcher revised their research question mid-process in response to what participants told them — and what that revision made possible.

---

## Part 2 brief

**Try:** Write your one-page living blueprint: research question, methodology, significance. Define clear success criteria. Then write one sentence about what you would do if the research found something that required changing the question.

**Share:** Share your blueprint with a peer. Ask: what would this change for the people it's meant to serve?

**Reflect:** What would it take for your organisation to accept a research proposal that is explicitly designed to adapt as it progresses?
