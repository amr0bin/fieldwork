# Week 2

**Pillar:** All three  
**Phase:** Foundations & Thesis Discovery

---

## Heading — the provocation

**Three pillars treated as silos produce three kinds of failure.**

---

## Three subheadings — the moves

**1. What the system currently does**  
Most organisations treat accessibility, disability justice, and pedagogy as separate departments, separate compliance functions, or separate professional specialisations. Accessibility is IT's problem. Disability justice is HR's problem. Pedagogy is the instructional designer's problem. None of them speak to each other at the level of the design.

**2. Whose interests that serves**  
Keeping the pillars separate protects professional silos. It also means the learner who needs all three to be working simultaneously — and they all do — is never the primary organising principle of the design.

**3. What becomes possible**  
When the three pillars are treated as a single, integrated hypothesis, the question shifts from "are we compliant?" to "is this learner included?" That is a much harder question. It is also the right one.

---

## Part 1 brief

**Why:** Introduce the three pillars and explain why treating them as a single, interdependent hypothesis is the central claim of the curriculum. Show concretely what it looks like when a system has strong accessibility infrastructure but no disability justice compass, or strong disability justice values but no adaptive pedagogy.

**See:** A program or organisation that initially operated with one or two pillars and experienced a specific, traceable failure — a learner who slipped through, a policy that held but excluded, a curriculum that complied but didn't reach.

---

## Part 2 brief

**Try:** Audit your current environment against all three pillars. Where is each one present? Where is it missing? Where do two pillars appear but the third is absent?

**Share:** Share your audit with a peer. Ask: where do you see the same pattern in your environment?

**Reflect:** What would it take, in your organisation, for all three pillars to be the responsibility of the same person — or the same conversation?
