# Phase 1 — Foundations & Thesis Discovery

**Weeks 1–6 · Goal: Align personal interest with the thesis and establish the research problem**

Phase 1 is where the inquiry becomes real. Its purpose is not to teach concepts in the abstract — it is to move readers from a vague sense that something is wrong in their environment to a specific, researchable question they are genuinely motivated to pursue. By Week 6, a participant should have a concrete statement of intent, a scoped research question, and a one-page living proposal. What they do not yet have is answers. That is exactly right.

**What your writing must do in Phase 1**

Lower the threshold of entry. The reader who arrives at Week 1 may be sceptical, time-pressed, or uncertain whether their context is "high-stakes enough" for serious inquiry. Every Phase 1 post must make the case that their specific environment — however imperfect, however resource-constrained — is the research site. The inquiry begins where the practitioner already is.

Establish the vocabulary without jargon. The three pillars, the policy-practice gap, the statement of intent, the practitioner-researcher stance — these are introduced across Phase 1. Introduce each term once, define it through use rather than definition, and let it recur.
