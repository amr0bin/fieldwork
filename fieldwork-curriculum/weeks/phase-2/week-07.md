# Week 7

**Pillar:** Accessibility  
**Phase:** Literature Review & Theoretical Scaffolding

---

## Heading — the provocation

**What the law requires and what it misses are two different questions.**

---

## Keywords

- Accessible Canada Act (ACA)
- AODA
- Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA)
- Legal floor
- Legislative intent vs. lived experience

---

## Key sentences

| Subheading | Load-bearing claim |
|------------|-------------------|
| What the system does | Legal frameworks establish what organisations must do; they do not establish what learners must experience. The gap between these two standards is where most exclusion lives. |
| Whose interests | The legal floor was negotiated by and for the people with access to legal and political processes — and that access has never been evenly distributed. |
| What becomes possible | When legal compliance is treated as the beginning of the design conversation rather than the end of it, the question shifts from "are we covered?" to "who are we still failing?" |

---

## Part 1 brief

**Why:** Introduce the legal framework — ACA, AODA, ADA — as the baseline the field must start from, not the destination it should be aiming for. Name what the law requires and specifically what it leaves open. Apply legal literacy as a design tool: ask whose exclusion might be written into the standard itself.

**See:** An organisation that audited its legal compliance and found it fully met — and then asked what the law left open. What did that second question find?

---

## Part 2 brief

**Try:** Audit your environment against the legal baseline most relevant to your jurisdiction. Identify what the law requires. Then identify three things the law does not address that your learners actually need.

**Share:** Share your audit with a peer. What gaps did you each find that the law leaves open?

**Reflect:** If the legal floor were raised tomorrow to require what you believe genuine inclusion demands, what would your organisation need to change first?
