# Phase 2 — Literature Review & Theoretical Scaffolding

**Weeks 7–12 · Goal: Situate the inquiry within existing legal, social, and pedagogical frameworks**

Phase 2 asks the practitioner-researcher to leave their own environment temporarily and look at the field — what the law requires, what the technical standards establish, what disability justice demands, what emergent learning theory makes possible, and what neuroeducational evidence confirms. The purpose is not to master the literature for its own sake, but to find the specific gaps that make the reader's inquiry necessary.

**What your writing must do in Phase 2**

Bring theory into contact with practice. Phase 2 posts should not feel like literature reviews. They should feel like a practitioner discovering that the language they have been using doesn't capture what they have been observing — and finding, in the literature, a more precise vocabulary. By Week 12, the reader should be able to articulate a gap statement: where current inclusive design falls short, and why that gap is what their inquiry is specifically positioned to address.
