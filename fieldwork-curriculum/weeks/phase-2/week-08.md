## Heading — the provocation

**A checklist that passes can still exclude.**

---

## Keywords

- WCAG 2.1
- Perceivable, operable, understandable, robust
- Technical compliance
- Pedagogical usability
- Cultural alienation

---

## Key sentences

| Subheading | Load-bearing claim |
|------------|-------------------|
| What the system does | WCAG 2.1 establishes whether content can be accessed; it does not establish whether it can be understood, whether it is culturally relevant, or whether it actually teaches anything. |
| Whose interests | A checklist is designed to be verifiable, not to be experienced. The learner's experience is not on the checklist. |
| What becomes possible | When technical standards are treated as necessary grammar rather than sufficient design, the question becomes: what does this content do for the learner who can now access it? |

---

## Part 1 brief / Part 2 brief

**Why:** Deep dive into WCAG 2.1 — perceivable, operable, understandable, robust. Establish these as necessary grammar. Then introduce the critical distinction: a piece of content can pass every WCAG criterion and still be pedagogically useless or culturally alienating for a specific learner.

**Try:** Run a WCAG scan on one piece of digital content you own. Identify what passes. Then identify what passes and is still inaccessible in practice — to whom, and why.
