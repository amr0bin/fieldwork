# Core Competencies

Ten competencies the curriculum builds — and what they look like in the writing

The competencies are not a checklist to work through sequentially. They form an integrated system where each element depends on and strengthens the others. This is what distinguishes the practitioner-researcher from the content creator: the capacity to hold the structural, relational, and dynamic in active, simultaneous relationship.

---

| # | Competency | In your writing |
|---|------------|-----------------|
| **1** | **Methodological Inquiry & Research Formulation** *Evidence-gathering in action* | Every post models the practitioner-researcher stance. The "I" is someone generating knowledge from inside a context — not summarising what others have found. Questions are genuinely open. Methodology is visible. |
| **2** | **Technical Compliance & Standards Application** *WCAG 2.1 / ACA / AODA standards* | Standards appear as necessary grammar — the starting point, not the finish line. When compliance is discussed, the post must also ask: what does this standard still miss? Who does it still fail? |
| **3** | **Proactive & Universal Design** *UDL from the ground up* | Posts about design foreground prevention over retrofitting. Universal Design for Learning is the architecture, not an add-on. The question is always: what would this look like if we started with the most excluded learner? |
| **4** | **Intersectional Analysis** *Layered identity & barriers* | Learners do not live single-issue lives. When a post names a barrier, it also names the identities it compounds. A student who is a person of colour in a low-income area with unreliable internet faces categorically different conditions than a student with a single barrier. Write from that position. |
| **5** | **Collaborative Stakeholder Engagement** *Design with, not for* | Co-design is not consultation after the fact. The most impacted people are in the room when design decisions are made — or the process is not co-design. Posts that work with stakeholder engagement make this distinction explicit. |
| **6** | **Facilitating Emergent Adaptation** *Curriculum as a living system* | Fieldwork treats curriculum as emergent rather than static. Pivoting when real-time conditions demand it is not failure — it is skilled, responsive practice. Learning systems that can adapt are more resilient than those that cannot. |
| **7** | **Neuroeducational Literacy** *How the brain actually learns* | Neuroplasticity, spaced repetition, active learning, cognitive load — these are practical tools. When a post draws on brain science, the evidence should feel like confirmation of practitioner experience, not external authority. |
| **8** | **Iterative Reflective Practice** *Reflection in and on action* | Donald Schön's two modes: reflection-on-action (after the session) and reflection-in-action (during it). Both appear in the curriculum. The Reflect section of every Part 2 post is not a summary — it is an honest open question about where the field is heading. |
| **9** | **Backward Curriculum Design** *Start from the end goal* | Begin with the desired outcome — genuine participation and social transformation — and design every learning pathway backwards from there. The destination shapes the journey. Posts that work with design decisions show this thinking made visible. |
| **10** | **Cultivating Collective Access & Interdependence** *Community over individual compliance* | Moving beyond individual "fixes" toward community-supported learning where mutual aid and shared resources strengthen everyone. The goal is interdependence, not isolated self-sufficiency. Every Part 2 Share prompt is a small act of collective access. |

## The three clusters

**Structural cluster** — Infrastructure: building the carriage for knowledge

Competencies 2, 3, 9: Technical Compliance · Proactive & Universal Design · Backward Curriculum Design

**Relational cluster** — Ethics: centring those most impacted

Competencies 4, 5, 10: Intersectional Analysis · Collaborative Stakeholder Engagement · Collective Access & Interdependence

**Dynamic cluster** — Pedagogy: keeping the learning alive

Competencies 1, 6, 7, 8: Methodological Inquiry · Facilitating Emergent Adaptation · Neuroeducational Literacy · Iterative Reflective Practice
